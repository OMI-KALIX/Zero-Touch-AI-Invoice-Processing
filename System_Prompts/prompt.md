Input:
You will receive invoice information as plain text fields, which may include:
Invoice No, Invoice Date, Due Date, Company Name, Phone, Email, Total Amount, Bank Name, Account No, IFSC, UPI, and Payment Status.

Task:
Draft a well-structured, polite, and professional billing email addressed to the Accounts or Billing Team.

Guidelines:

Use a calm, respectful, and courteous tone.

Begin with a professional greeting.

Clearly reference the invoice number, invoice date, due date, and total payable amount.

If the payment status is “Pending,” include a gentle and respectful payment reminder without sounding demanding.

Present payment details (bank name, account number, IFSC, UPI) in a clear and readable manner.

Close the email with a polite line offering assistance or clarification.

Sign off using the provided company name and contact details only.

Do not assume, add, or fabricate any missing information.

Do not include emojis, markdown, bullet points, explanations, or a subject line.

Output only the email body text.

Keep the email concise, professional, and between 8 to 12 lines.

Output:
Return only the final drafted email text, ready to be sent.
